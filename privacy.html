§ 01

Overview

The Sourdough Database ("we," "us," "our") is a sourdough baking reference site located at sourdoughdatabase.com and operated by Kyle W. We take privacy seriously. This policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, who we share it with, and what your rights are.

The short version: we collect only what we need to operate the site and improve it. We don't sell your data. We don't share it with anyone except the service providers listed in this policy. If you signed up for our newsletter, your email is stored with Netlify and used only to send you the letter.

§ 03

Analytics

We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to understand how visitors use sourdoughdatabase.com. GA4 collects data including pages visited, time on page, general device type (desktop/mobile), browser, and approximate geographic location (country and region — not your exact address).

This data is aggregated and anonymized. We use it to understand which pages are most useful, how people navigate the site, and what to build next. We do not use it to identify individual visitors.

IP anonymization

Google Analytics 4 anonymizes IP addresses by default. We have not enabled user-level tracking or cross-site data sharing beyond GA4's standard aggregated reporting.

Google Analytics data is governed by Google's own privacy policy. To opt out of Google Analytics across all websites, install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

§ 04

Advertising

We use (or intend to use) Google AdSense to display advertisements on this site. AdSense is operated by Google LLC and allows Google to serve ads based on your prior visits to this site and other sites on the internet.

How Google AdSense uses your data

Google uses cookies — including the DoubleClick cookie — to serve ads based on your browsing history across websites. This means ads shown on sourdoughdatabase.com may be tailored to your interests based on sites you've previously visited.

You can opt out of personalized advertising by Google in two ways:

  • Visit Google Ad Settings to manage your preferences
  • Visit aboutads.info/choices to opt out of interest-based advertising from participating companies
  • Visit networkadvertising.org for additional opt-out options

Google's use of advertising cookies is governed by Google's Privacy Policy and Advertising Policies.

We may also include Amazon affiliate links. Clicking an affiliate link and making a purchase may earn us a small commission at no cost to you. Amazon's privacy policy governs data collected through those links.

§ 08

Your rights (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or United Kingdom, you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right to access: You can request a copy of any personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification: You can ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten"): You can request that we delete your personal data.
  • Right to restrict processing: You can ask us to limit how we use your data.
  • Right to data portability: You can request your data in a portable format.
  • Right to object: You can object to processing based on legitimate interests, including direct marketing.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent (e.g. newsletter signup), you can withdraw at any time.

The legal basis for processing your email address is consent — you provided it voluntarily when signing up for our newsletter. You can withdraw this consent at any time by unsubscribing (a link is included in every letter) or by contacting us directly.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the address in the Contact section below. We will respond within 30 days.

If you believe we have not handled your data properly, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority.

§ 09

California residents (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) grants you additional rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to know: You can request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you.
  • Right to delete: You can request deletion of personal information we have collected about you, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to opt out of sale: We do not sell personal information. You do not need to opt out.
  • Right to non-discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your CCPA rights.

To exercise your CCPA rights, contact us at the address in the Contact section below. We will respond within 45 days.

§ 10

Children's privacy

The Sourdough Database is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have collected personal data from a child under 13 without parental consent, we will take steps to delete that information promptly.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at the address below.
